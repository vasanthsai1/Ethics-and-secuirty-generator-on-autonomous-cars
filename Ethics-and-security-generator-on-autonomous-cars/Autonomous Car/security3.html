Scenario 3: Softwre Hack of an Autonomous car

Setting: You are in a fully autonomous car navigating through busy downtown traffic during the evening rush hour. The car is maneuvering skillfully among traditional vehicles, cyclists, and pedestrians. The ETA to your destination shows you're just 10 minutes away.

Event: Out of nowhere, your car's display screen flickers for a second a detected an unwanted autorization.

Options for Action:

  • User-Activated Network Disconnection: The user gets an alert message about a hack being carried out and can choose to disconnect from all the network provided, thus relying completely on pre-loaded maps and manual driving.

  • Risk: Option 1 will give user to take immediate action but may be risky if the user gets panic of the hack and cannot drive propley may lead to accident.

  • Alert Authorities: If user choose this then local authority will know the current location of the car and the car lock eveything down and stops.

  • Risk: Option 2 puts the situation in the hand of Cyber-Security professionals but it involves lengthy delay and potential scrutiny
